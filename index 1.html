Discover Yala's Wildlife

  • Yala National Park: Yala National Park, located in the southeastern corner of Sri Lanka, is one of the country's most renowned natural sanctuaries. Spanning over 978 square kilometers, this verdant expanse is celebrated for its diverse ecosystems, including dry forests, grasslands, lagoons, and wetlands. It offers visitors a unique opportunity to immerse themselves in the rich biodiversity and majestic beauty of Sri Lanka's wilderness.
  • A Haven for Wildlife: The Yala Forest is home to an extraordinary array of wildlife. It boasts one of the highest densities of leopards in the world, making it a prime destination for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers. Apart from the elusive leopard, the park shelters elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles, and spotted deer. Birdlife in Yala is equally mesmerizing, with over 200 recorded species, including the vibrant painted stork, peacocks, and endemic Sri Lankan junglefowl. Yala’s unique ecosystems support a wide range of flora and fauna, many of which are endemic to the region. The arid climate and seasonal monsoons contribute to the formation of waterholes and lagoons that become lifelines for animals during the dry months. The juxtaposition of dense forests and open plains ensures that visitors are constantly treated to spectacular and varying landscapes.
  • The Yala Safari Experience: The Yala Safari Service is a gateway to the wonders of the national park. Organized tours take visitors on an unforgettable journey into the heart of the wild. The safari experience is tailored to suit diverse preferences, with options ranging from half-day excursions to full-day adventures. Each safari is guided by experienced trackers and naturalists who share their extensive knowledge of the park’s flora and fauna. The expertise of these guides ensures that visitors have the best chance to spot iconic wildlife while also learning about the ecological significance of Yala. Safaris are conducted in specially designed 4x4 vehicles, providing comfort and safety as visitors traverse rugged terrain.
